Ica



The city of Ica is the capital of the Ica Region and is the most important wine producing area in Peru. Traveling south from Lima on the Pan-American Highway, after approximately a four-hour bus ride you will see the fishing village of Pisco. From Pisco it takes us an hour and a half to get to Ica.



Turism

Ica is not only famous for its wines, but also for its huge sand dunes - formed by the Paracas winds in this part of the Peruvian coast - and last but not least, for the Huacachina lagoon. This attraction is a natural oasis 4 kilometers from Ica, in the middle of the sand dunes.









Arequipa



Arequipa is a World Heritage Site according to Unesco. Known as the city of white stone, it offers snow-capped destinations such as the Ampato, Chachani and Misti peaks, along with deep canyons. Among them, Cotahuasi and Colca, whose beauty and impact are impressive.



The 5 best reasons to visit Arequipa

  1. World Heritage Site
  2. A very beautiful cathedral
  3. The incredible Colca Canyon
  4. Conquer a volcano
  5. Local food

If you are a nature lover and enjoy being surrounded by extensive green areas, without a doubt the Peruvian Amazon is one of the destinations that should be on your agenda, yes or yes. And it is that this part of Peru offers unbeatable options to have a direct connection with the local fauna and flora.

They will welcome you with open arms and will be more than happy for you to get to know these lands full of forests, exotic animals, indigenous peoples and more.

La Libertad

A region in which the activities on ancient history, contact with nature and relaxation for all those who deserve a well-deserved rest are very well balanced. This destination always has its doors open to receive that tourist who wants to know its lands, full of tradition and unique ecosystems.


If your intention is to pack your towel, sandals, swimwear and sunscreen in your suitcases, you are aiming for the perfect destination. In the spas of La Libertad you will find long stretches of fine sand, with calm waters for those who want to take a dip or perfect waves for those who want to learn to surf.
